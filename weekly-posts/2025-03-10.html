The Kelvin-Helmholtz Clouds

Phenomenon: Wave-like cloud formations that look like ocean waves rolling through the sky.

Physics: Two layers of air moving at different speeds create shear at their interface. When the Richardson number drops below 1/4, the interface becomes unstable and rolls up into breaking-wave shapes.

Ri = gΔρh / ρ(ΔU)²

Where to see it: Mountain ranges after cold fronts, especially on the lee side where wind shear is strongest.
