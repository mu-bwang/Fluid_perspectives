Week of March 10, 2025 Nature Publication

Waves on the Orion Molecular Cloud

Real Phenomenon: Astronomers using the JWST (James Webb Space Telescope) and VLA (Very Large Array) have detected massive Kelvin-Helmholtz-like waves at the surface of the Orion molecular cloud, where new stars are forming. These are the largest-scale shear instabilities ever observed in the interstellar medium.

What's happening: Gas heated and ionized by massive newborn stars is being blown over the denser, cooler molecular cloud. The shear between these two layers—moving at velocities of ~20 km/s—generates hydrodynamical instabilities that fragment the cloud, potentially triggering new star formation.

Why it matters: This is the first direct observation of Kelvin-Helmholtz instabilities at cloud boundaries in star-forming regions, confirming decades-old theoretical predictions about how stellar feedback disrupts molecular clouds.

Ri = gΔρh / ρ(ΔU)²

When Ri < 0.25, shear overcomes buoyancy → instability grows
